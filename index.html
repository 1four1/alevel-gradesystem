The Problem

The Grade Gap Is Real — and It's Not About Effort

Most A-Level students are doing everything they're supposed to. The problem isn't how hard they work. It's how they work.

📖

The Passive Revision Trap

Re-reading, highlighting, rewriting notes — it feels productive but produces near-zero retention. Students spend 15–25 hours a week on work that mostly doesn't stick.

📝

Mark Schemes Are Written in Code

"AO1, AO2, AO3." "Analyse and evaluate." Students read the mark scheme and still don't know what the examiner actually wanted — and they lose 1–2 grade boundaries as a result.

⏳

Feedback Arrives Too Late

Write an essay on Monday. Get feedback Thursday at the earliest. By then the student has written three more essays the same wrong way. Mistakes compound into habits.

🎯

No System for What to Study

Students revise what they feel comfortable with, not what the examiner will actually test. Without a priority map, time goes to the wrong places every single session.

"I know the content — I just don't know how to get the marks." — The most common thing A-Level students say
